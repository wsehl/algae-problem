Algae Problem

Some algal blooms are the result of an excess of nutrients (particularly phosphorus and nitrogen) into waters and higher concentrations of these nutrients in water cause increased growth of algae and green plants.

What is Eutrophication?

Eutrophication is when a body of water becomes over-enriched with minerals and nutrients that cause excessive algae growth. This process can lead to oxygen depletion in the reservoir.One example is "algal blooms" or a significant increase in phytoplankton in a body of water in response to increased nutrient levels. The main chemical elements contributing to eutrophication are phosphorus and nitrogen.

A look at aftermath

Microflora growth

The growth of microflora reduces the transparency of the water, the penetration depth of the sun's rays decreases, as a result of the lack of light, the death of benthic plants begins. The process of dying off of bottom aquatic plants entails the death of other organisms.

Soil

Anaerobic decomposition occurs in the oxygen-deprived bottom soil dead organisms with the formation of such strong poisons as phenols and hydrogen sulfide, and such a powerful "greenhouse gas" (in its effect in this respect, superior to carbon dioxide 25 times), as methane.

Photosynthesis

At night, photosynthesis in these plants does not occur, but the active process of respiration continues. In summer, in the early morning hours of warm days, the oxygen content in the upper water horizons decreases and the death of aerobic organisms that inhabit these horizons and demanding oxygen content is observed.

Michigan Studies

Scientists from the University of Michigan have developed a method to prevent eutrophication. In their project, they used buoys that constantly analyze the water.

We propose to modify this method by adding buoys the ability to emit ultrasonic waves, due to the appearance of bubbles with a vapor-gas mixture in the liquid. Bubble rupture is accompanied by the destruction of nearby algal cells. Previously, a similar method was used so far only as an experiment in Lake Ladoga, but in practice it was not used due to problems with the transportation of equipment, but such autonomous installations powered by solar energy completely solve this problem.
